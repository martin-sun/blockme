## T2 Corporation Income Tax Return – Filing Requirements and Deadlines

### T2 Return Types

#### T2 Corporation Income Tax Return
- Nine-page return available for any corporation
- Standard filing option for all Canadian corporations

#### T2 Short Return
- Two-page return plus Schedule 1 (Net Income/Loss for Income Tax Purposes)
- May require additional schedules:
  - Schedule 8 (Capital Cost Allowance)
  - Schedule 50 (Shareholder Information)

**Eligibility for T2 Short Return:**

1. **Canadian-Controlled Private Corporations (CCPCs)** throughout the tax year with:
   - Nil net income OR loss for income tax purposes

2. **Tax-exempt corporations** under section 149 (e.g., non-profit organizations)

**Additional requirements for T2 Short Return:**
- Permanent establishment in only one province or territory (see page 98)
- No refundable tax credits claimed (except refund of instalments paid)
- No taxable dividends received or paid
- Reporting in Canadian currency
- No Ontario transitional tax debit
- No amount calculated under section 34.2

> **Note:** If corporation doesn't meet eligibility criteria, file regular T2 return

### Filing Deadlines

**Standard deadline:** File within six months of tax year-end

**Deadline calculation rules:**
- If tax year ends on last day of month: File by last day of sixth month after year-end
- If tax year ends on other day: File by same day of sixth month after year-end

| Tax Year-End | Filing Deadline |
|--------------|-----------------|
| March 31     | September 30    |
| April 30     | October 31      |
| June 30      | December 31     |
| September 23 | March 23        |
| October 2    | April 2         |

**Weekend/Holiday Rule:** When due date falls on Saturday, Sunday, or CRA-recognized public holiday, return is considered on time if received or postmarked by next business day. Provincial holidays may affect deadlines.

> **Critical:** File on time to avoid penalties (see page 15)
> 
> **Refund Limitation:** Must file within three years of tax year-end to receive tax refund

### Re-appropriation of T2 Statute-Barred Credits

Under subsection 221.2(1), Minister may re-appropriate T2 statute-barred credits to established debts on same business number.

**Request Process:**
- Submit completed Form RC431 (Request for Re-appropriation of T2 Statute-barred Credits)
- Include all supporting documents
- Separate form for each business number
- Alternative: Use "Enquiries service" in My Business Account

**Reference:** canada.ca/t2-reappropriation

### Payment Requirements

#### Instalment Payments

**Requirement:** Corporations must pay monthly or quarterly instalments unless total of Part I, Part VI, Part VI.1, and Part XIII.1 taxes payable is $3,000 or less for current or previous year.

**Monthly Instalment Due Dates:**
- Due last day of each complete month of tax year
- First payment due one month minus one day from tax year start
- Subsequent payments due same day each following month

**Quarterly Option:** Eligible small CCPCs can pay quarterly (see Guide T7B–Corp)

**Payment Methods:**
1. Online banking/telephone service (Canadian banks/credit unions)
2. CRA My Payment service (canada.ca/cra-my-payment)
3. Pre-authorized debit (PAD) through My Business Account
4. "Proceed to pay" button in My Business Account
5. Third-party providers (credit card, Interac e-Transfer, PayPal) - fees apply
6. In-person at banks/credit unions or Canada Post (with remittance voucher)

> **Note:** Remittance vouchers include QR code for Canada Post payments

#### Balance-Due Day

**Standard:** Two months after tax year-end (except Part III and Part XII.6 taxes)

**Extended Deadline (Three months):** Applies when:
- Corporation is CCPC throughout tax year
- Claiming small business deduction for current year OR claimed in previous year
- AND either:
  - Previous year taxable income ≤ business limit (non-associated corporations)
  - Total taxable incomes of associated corporations ≤ total business limits (associated corporations)

> **Note:** For balance-due determination, taxable income calculated before loss carrybacks

**Special Rules:** Apply to new corporations after amalgamation or parent corporations receiving subsidiary assets (see Guide T7B–Corp)

**References:** Sections 125 and 157

### Partnership Rules – Section 34.2

#### Adjusted Stub Period Accrual (ASPA) Regime

Corporations may need to accrue additional income for partnerships when:
- Partnership fiscal period begins in corporation's tax year
- Partnership fiscal period ends in following tax year

**ASPA Requirements:** Corporation must include income if:
- Has significant interest (>10%) in partnership at end of last fiscal period ending in tax year
- Another partnership fiscal period begins in tax year and ends after corporation's tax year
- Entitled to share of partnership income/loss/capital gains at year-end

**Reporting Requirements:**
- Use Schedule 71 (single-tier partnerships) or Schedule 72 (multi-tier partnerships) for calculations
- File Schedule 73 (Income Inclusion Summary) with return
- Schedules 1, 6, and 7 affected by section 34.2 amounts

**References:** Sections 34.2, 34.3, and 249.1

### Penalties

#### Late Filing Penalties

**Standard Penalty:**
- 5% of unpaid tax due on filing deadline
- Plus 1% of unpaid tax for each complete month late (maximum 12 months)

**Increased Penalty (if CRA demanded filing AND penalty assessed in previous 3 years):**
- 10% of unpaid tax when due
- Plus 2% for each complete month late (maximum 20 months)

**References:** Subsections 162(1) and 162(2)

#### Non-Resident Corporations

Penalty equals greater of:
- Amount calculated under subsections 162(1) or (2)
- Greater of $100 OR $25 per complete day late (maximum 100 days)

**Reference:** Subsection 162(2.1)

#### Large Corporations

**Definition:** Total taxable capital employed in Canada > $10 million (corporation and related corporations)

**Penalty for Late Filing:** (per complete month, maximum 40 months)
- 0.0005% of taxable capital employed in Canada (Part I.3 definition)
- 0.25% of Part VI tax payable [before subsection 190.1(3) deductions]

**Identification:** Answer yes to line 233 on page 2 of return

**Required Schedules for Capital Determination:**
- Schedule 33 (Large Corporations)
- Schedule 34 (Financial Institutions)
- Schedule 35 (Large Insurance Corporations)

**Provincial Requirements:**
- Newfoundland and Labrador: Schedule 305 (Financial Institutions)
- Nova Scotia: Schedule 352 (Financial Institutions) for tax years starting after October 31, 2021

**Reference:** Section 235

#### Mandatory Electronic Filing Non-Compliance

**Penalty:** $1,000 for corporations required to file electronically but fail to comply

**Reference:** Subsection 162(7.2)

#### Failure to Report Income

**Penalty applies when:**
- Unreported amount ≥ $500
- Occurs in current year or any of three previous years
- Not liable under subsection 163(2) for same amount

**Penalty amount:** Lesser of:
- 10% of unreported amount
- 50% of difference between understated tax and tax withheld related to unreported amount

**References:** Subsections 163(1) and 163(1.1)

#### False Statements or Omissions

**Penalty:** Greater of $100 OR 50% of understated tax amount

**Reference:** Subsection 163(2)

> **Note:** Cannot charge both false statement penalty and failure to report penalty for same amount

#### Third-Party Misrepresentation

**Penalty applies** when person advises/helps file false return or knowingly allows false tax information

**References:** IC01-1, Section 163.2

#### Tax Debt Avoidance (Proposed Changes)

**New Supplementary Rule:** (for transactions after April 15, 2024)
- Property transferred by tax debtor deemed transferred to transferee
- Joint and several liability for participants in tax debt avoidance planning
- Extended penalty application

**Penalty:** Lesser of:
- 50% of tax attempted to be avoided
- $100,000 plus amount person entitled to receive regarding planning

**Reference:** Subsection 160(6)

#### Other Penalties

Common penalties for:
- Late or incomplete instalment payments
- Failure to provide information on required forms:
  - Form T106 (Non-Arm's Length Transactions with Non-Residents)
  - T5013 FIN/T5013 SUM (Partnership Returns)
  - T5018 SUM (Contract Payments Summary)
  - Forms T1134, T1135, T1141, T1142 (Foreign Reporting)
  - Form T661 (SR&ED Claim - Part 9)

**References:** Sections 162 and 163.1

### Taxpayer Relief Provisions

**CRA discretion** to cancel/waive penalties and interest for circumstances beyond taxpayer control

**Time Limitation:** Relief limited to periods ending within 10 calendar years before request year

**Request Methods:**
1. **Online:**
   - My Account: Accounts and payments → Request relief
   - My Business Account/Represent a Client: Select program → Request relief
2. **Form RC4288** (Request for Taxpayer Relief):
   - Submit online through My Account/MyBA/Represent a Client
   - Mail to designated office

**Information:** canada.ca/penalty-interest-relief

**References:** Subsection 220(3.1), IC07-1R

### Voluntary Disclosures Program (VDP)

**Purpose:** Correct previously filed returns or file missed returns before CRA enforcement action

**Program Types:**
1. **Limited Program:** For intentional tax avoidance (limited relief)
2. **General Program:** For other disclosures

**Application Process:** Must be voluntary, complete, and include payment of estimated taxes
