## Part 1 of Schedule 5 â€“ Allocation of Taxable Income

### When to Complete Schedule 5
You must complete Part 1 of Schedule 5 if you or your partnerships had a permanent establishment in more than one province or territory. 

**Implementation Requirements:**
- Complete columns A to F for each province or territory with a permanent establishment
- If no taxable income exists, complete only columns A, B, and D
- Track permanent establishment locations throughout the tax year

> **Note:** This also applies to corporations with permanent establishments in Quebec or Alberta.

### Provincial/Territorial Tax Assessment
The CRA assesses provincial or territorial income taxes on the amount of taxable income allocated to each province or territory. 

**Reference Documents:**
- Regulation 402
- Income Tax Folio S4-F3-C2, Provincial Income Allocation

### Special Rules for Partnership Members
Special rules apply when a corporation is a member of a partnership with permanent establishments in multiple jurisdictions.

**Required Documentation:**
- Guide T4068, Guide for the Partnership Information Return (T5013 Forms)
- Form T5013 SCH 5, Allocation of Salaries and Wages, and Gross Revenue for Multiple Jurisdictions

**Implementation Note:** Whether or not the partnership filed a T5013, partner corporations must report their permanent establishments and allocable revenue and salaries on their own Schedule 5, including partnership allocations.

#### Example: Partnership Allocation
If a partner has a 50% share of partnership income:
- Include 50% of the gross revenue from T5013 SCH 5 in its T2 SCH 5 gross revenue
- Report 50% of partnership salaries and wages on the T2 SCH 5

### General Allocation Method
The standard allocation formula uses:
- Gross revenue
- Salaries and wages

**Reference:** Regulation 402 for detailed gross revenue allocation rules

### Exclusions from Gross Revenue
**Do NOT include:**
- Interest on bonds, debentures, or mortgages
- Dividends on shares of capital stock
- Rents or royalties from property not part of principal business operations

### Allocation of Salaries and Wages
**Implementation Rules:**
- Allocate to the permanent establishment where employees worked
- Exclude commissions paid to non-employees (unless performing employee-equivalent services)
- Apply Regulation 402.1 deeming rules for central paymaster arrangements
