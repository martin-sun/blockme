## Manufacturing and Processing Profits Deduction (MPPD)

### Eligibility for Small Manufacturing Corporations
To qualify as a small manufacturing corporation, all of the following requirements must be met:
- Activities during the year were primarily manufacturing or processing
- Active business income (including that of any associated Canadian corporations) did not exceed $200,000
- No engagement in activities specifically excluded from manufacturing and processing (as defined in subsection 125.1(3)), such as:
  - Farming, fishing, logging
  - Construction
  - Operating an oil or gas well
  - Extracting petroleum, natural gas, or minerals
  - Processing ore
  - Producing industrial minerals
- No active business was carried on outside Canada at any time during the year

### Calculation Methods
- **Small Manufacturing Corporations**: Complete Part 1 of Schedule 27 to calculate MPPD on entire adjusted business income
- **Other Corporations**: Complete Part 2 of Schedule 27 using the basic formula for Canadian manufacturing and processing profits
- **Electricity/Steam Producers**: Complete Parts 10 to 13 of Schedule 27
- **Zero-Emission Technology Manufacturers**: Complete Parts 14 to 17 of Schedule 27

### Adjusted Business Income Definition
A corporation's adjusted business income is its income from an active business carried on in Canada that exceeds its losses from similar businesses. For corporations involved in resource activities, adjusted business income must be reduced by:
- Net resource income
- Refund interest
- Portion of prescribed resource loss

### Implementation Notes
- Enter the amount of the manufacturing and processing profits deduction and zero-emission technology manufacturing deduction on line 616
- Reference: Sections 125.1 and 125.2, Regulation 5200, S4-F15-C1

## Investment Corporation Deduction

### Eligibility
Canadian public corporations that qualify as investment corporations (as defined in subsection 130(3)) can claim this deduction.

### Calculation
The deduction equals 20% of the taxable income for the year that exceeds the taxed capital gains for the year.

### Implementation Notes
- Enter taxed capital gains on line 624
- Enter the deduction amount on line 620
- Reference: Section 130

## Federal Foreign Non-Business Income Tax Credit

### Purpose
This credit prevents double taxation of non-business income earned in foreign countries that was taxed by those countries. It's available to:
- Canadian residents
- Authorized foreign banks on their Canadian banking business from foreign sources

### Eligible Income Types
Foreign non-business income includes:
- Dividends
- Interest
- Capital gains

Exclusions:
- Dividends received from foreign affiliates
- Income from operating a business in a foreign country
- Foreign tax paid on income exempt from tax in Canada under an income tax treaty

### Alternative Treatment
Under subsection 20(12), instead of claiming the credit, a corporation can deduct from income all or any part of non-business income tax paid to a foreign country.

### Implementation Notes
- Calculate using Schedule 21, Part 1
- Calculate credits for each country separately
- Enter the total allowable credit (or lesser amount) on line 632
- Any remaining foreign non-business income tax can be claimed as a provincial or territorial foreign tax credit
- Reference: Subsection 126(1), S5-F2-C1

## Federal Foreign Business Income Tax Credit

### Purpose
This credit prevents double taxation for corporations that pay foreign tax on income or profits earned from operating a business in a foreign country.

### Implementation Notes
- Calculate using Schedule 21, Part 2
- Calculate credits for each country separately
- Enter the total allowable credits (or lesser amount) on line 636
- Unlike non-business income tax, excess foreign business income tax cannot be claimed as a provincial or territorial credit
- Reference: Subsection 126(2), S5-F2-C1

### Carryback and Carryforward Provisions
- Carryback: 3 previous tax years
- Carryforward: 10 tax years
- Can only be used to reduce Part I tax on income from the same foreign country

## Eligible Canadian Bank Deduction

- Reference: Subsection 95(2.43), Section 125.21

## Federal Qualifying Environmental Trust (QET) Tax Credit

### Eligibility
Corporations that are beneficiaries under a qualifying environmental trust can claim this credit.

### QET Definition
A QET is a trust that:
- Has trustees limited to:
  - Federal or provincial Crown, or
  - Canadian-resident corporations licensed to provide trustee services to the public
- Is maintained only to fund reclamation of sites in Canada used for:
  - Mining operations
  - Extraction of clay, peat, sand, shale, or aggregates
  - Waste deposits
  - Pipeline operations (for trusts created after 2011)
- Is required to be maintained under federal/provincial law, Crown contracts, or tribunal orders
- Is not an excluded trust (as defined in subsection 211.6(1))

### Implementation Notes
- Credit equals Part XII.4 tax payable by the trust (currently 15% rate)
- Enter credit up to the amount of Part I tax otherwise payable on line 648
- Enter any unused amount on line 792 (page 9)
- Reference: Section 127.41

## Investment Tax Credit (ITC)

### Purpose
Corporations can claim ITCs to reduce Part I tax, with some credits being fully or partially refundable.

### Calculation Method
ITCs are calculated by applying specified percentages to:
- Cost of acquiring certain property
- Certain expenditures

### Important Considerations
- Reduce capital cost of property or expenditure by any government or non-government assistance received
- GST/HST input tax credits or rebates are considered government assistance
- Property is not considered acquired for ITC purposes until it becomes available for use

### Implementation Notes
- Calculate using Schedule 31
- Reference available-for-use rule on page 46
- Reference: Subsections 13(26) to 13(32) and 127(11.2)

### Qualified Investments and Expenditures
A. Cost of acquiring qualified property
A.1 Cost of acquiring qualified resource property (carry-forward amounts only)
B. SR&ED qualified expenditure pool
