# Section 80

**Chapter 80**

## Provincial and Territorial Tax Credits

### Ontario Tax Credits

#### Ontario Interactive Digital Media Tax Credit
**Eligibility Requirements:**
- Must not be a prescribed labour-sponsored venture capital corporation during the tax year
- Must carry on business through a permanent establishment in Ontario developing digital games
- Cannot be a corporation whose primary activity is providing services of a single individual who owns all shares

**Application Process:**
1. Apply online to Ontario Creates for a certificate of eligibility before claiming
2. Ontario Creates issues certificate indicating estimated credit amount
3. Only one certificate issued per tax year for all eligible products
4. Apply within 18 months of tax year end when development completed

**Claim Requirements:**
- File certificate of eligibility (or copy) with T2 return
- Complete Schedule 560 for each eligible product
- Enter total credit on line 462 of Schedule 5

**Example:** A game development corporation in Ontario with $500,000 in eligible development expenses may qualify for this credit after receiving certification from Ontario Creates.

#### Ontario Book Publishing Tax Credit
**Credit Details:**
- 30% refundable credit on qualifying expenditures
- Maximum $30,000 per literary work
- Eligible for Canadian-authored works in fiction, non-fiction, poetry, drama, biography, or children's categories

**Qualifying Expenditures:**
- Pre-production costs
- Marketing expenditures (12 months before/after publication)
- 50% of production costs
- 100% of digital format preparation costs
- 50% of digital format transfer costs

**Example:** An Ontario publisher spending $80,000 on qualifying expenditures for a Canadian novel could claim a $24,000 credit (30% of $80,000).

#### Ontario Innovation Tax Credit
**Credit Details:**
- 8% refundable credit on qualified expenditures incurred in Ontario
- Maximum annual expenditure limit of $3 million (shared among associated corporations)
- Limit begins to reduce at $500,000 federal taxable income and becomes nil at $800,000

**Eligibility Requirements:**
- Permanent establishment in Ontario
- Carried on SR&ED in Ontario
- Not exempt from tax under Part III of Taxation Act, 2007
- Eligible for federal investment tax credit under section 127
- Filed Form T661 and Schedule 31

**Example:** A corporation with $2 million in qualified Ontario SR&ED expenditures could claim a $160,000 credit (8% of $2 million).

### Manitoba Tax Credits

#### Manitoba Manufacturing Investment Tax Credit
**Credit Rates:**
| Date of Acquisition | Refundable Rate | Non-refundable Rate | Total Rate |
|---------------------|-----------------|---------------------|------------|
| After June 30, 2019 | 7%              | 1%                  | 8%         |
| Before July 1, 2019 | 8%              | 1%                  | 9%         |
| Before April 12, 2017 | 8%           | 2%                  | 10%        |

**Eligible Property:**
- New and used buildings, machinery, and equipment
- Made available for use in Manitoba primarily for manufacturing/processing goods for sale or lease
- Certain green energy equipment also eligible

**Carry Provisions:**
- Non-refundable credit: Carry back 3 years, carry forward 10 years
- File Schedule 381 within 12 months after return due date

#### Manitoba Research and Development Tax Credit
**Credit Details:**
- 15% of eligible expenditures
- 50% refundable for non-contract R&D, fully refundable for contract R&D with qualifying research institute
- Eligible expenditures: Current expenditures and capital expenditures (excluding buildings)

**Example:** A Manitoba corporation with $200,000 in eligible R&D expenditures could claim:
- $15,000 total credit (15% of $200,000)
- $7,500 refundable portion (50% for non-contract R&D)

#### Manitoba Paid Work Experience Tax Credit
**Credit Components:**
- Youth work experience hiring incentive: 25%, lifetime max $5,000
- Co-op student hiring incentive: 15%, lifetime max $5,000
- Co-op graduate hiring incentive: 15%, max $2,500
- Apprentice hiring incentive: 15-25%, max $5,000
- Journeyperson hiring incentive: 15%, max $5,000

**Example:** An employer hiring a high school apprentice in rural Manitoba could claim 25% of eligible wages up to $5,000.

### Saskatchewan Tax Credits

#### Saskatchewan Manufacturing and Processing Investment Tax Credit
**Credit Details:**
- 6% fully refundable credit on capital cost of qualified property
- Property must be used in Saskatchewan primarily for manufacturing/processing goods for lease or sale
- Corporations exempt under section 149 not eligible

**Claim Process:**
- File Schedule 402 with return
- Enter amount on line 644 of Schedule 5

#### Saskatchewan Research and Development Tax Credit
**Credit Structure:**
| Expenditures Incurred | Annual Expenditures | Non-CCPCs | CCPCs |
|-----------------------|---------------------|-----------|-------|
| After March 31, 2017 | $1 million or less | Non-refundable | Refundable |
| After March 31, 2017 | More than $1M, up to $10M | Non-refundable | Non-refundable |

**Example:** A CCPC with $800,000 in eligible SR&ED expenditures could claim a fully refundable $80,000 credit (10% of $800,000).

### British Columbia Tax Credits

#### British Columbia Film and Television Tax Credit
**Credit Components:**
- Basic tax credit: 35% of qualified BC labour expenditure
- Scriptwriting tax credit: 35% (after Feb 20, 2018)
- Regional tax credit: 12.5%
- Distant location regional tax credit: 6%
- Film training tax credit: 30%
- DAVE tax credit: 16%

**Eligibility Requirements:**
- Canadian-controlled taxable corporation with permanent establishment in BC
- Primarily carrying on film/video production business through permanent establishment in Canada
- Cannot claim if production services tax credit claimed for same production

**Documentation:**
- File eligibility certificate from Creative BC
- For completed productions: completion certificate and audited statement of production costs
- Form T1196 for each production

#### British Columbia Training Tax Credit
**Credit Components:**
- Basic tax credit: 20% of wages for first 24 months of non-Red Seal program (max $4,000)
- Completion tax credit: 15% for completing level 3 or 4 (max $2,500/$3,000)
- Enhanced tax credit: 5.5-30% for Indigenous persons or those qualifying for disability amount

**Example:** An employer paying $30,000 in wages to an apprentice in first 24 months could claim $6,000 (20% of $30,000).

### Yukon Tax Credits

#### Yukon Research and Development Tax Credit
**Credit Details:**
- 15% of eligible expenditures incurred in Yukon
- Additional 5% for expenditures paid to Yukon University
- Fully refundable but applied against taxes payable first
- File Schedule 442 within 18 months after tax year end

**Example:** A corporation with $100,000 in eligible Yukon R&D expenditures could claim $15,000 credit plus additional $500 if $10,000 paid to Yukon University.

### Northwest Territories and Nunavut Tax Credits

#### Political Contribution Tax Credits
**Northwest Territories:**
- Maximum $500 credit
- 100% of first $100 contributed
- 50% of next $800 contributed

**Nunavut:**
- Maximum $500 credit
- 100% of first $100 contributed
- 50% of next $800 contributed

**Note:** Contributions to political parties do not qualify in either territory.

## Federal Investment Tax Credits

### Clean Economy Investment Tax Credits

#### Carbon Capture, Utilization, and Storage (CCUS) ITC
**Credit Rates:**
- 60% for direct air capture equipment (30% after 2030)
- 50% for other capture equipment (25% after 2030)
- 37.5% for transportation, storage, and use equipment (18.75% after 2030)

**Labour Requirements:**
- Must elect to meet prevailing wage and apprenticeship requirements
- Otherwise, credit rate reduced by 10 percentage points

**Example:** A corporation investing $1 million in eligible capture equipment could claim $500,000 credit (50% rate).

#### Clean Technology ITC
**Credit Details:**
- 30% refundable credit on eligible clean technology property
- Property acquired and available for use after March 27, 2023 and before 2034
- Rate reduced to 15% in 2034, nil after 2034

**Eligible Property:**
- Zero-emission power generation technologies
- Storage equipment for zero-emission energy
- Non-road zero-emission vehicles
- Geothermal energy systems (unless used for fossil fuel co-production)

#### Clean Hydrogen ITC
**Credit Rates by Carbon Intensity:**
| Carbon Intensity (kg CO2eq/kg H2) | Credit Rate (2024-2033) | Credit Rate (2034) |
|-----------------------------------|-------------------------|-------------------|
| Less than 0.75                    | 40%                     | 20%               |
| 0.75 to <2.0                      | 25%                     | 12.5%             |
| 2.0 to <4.0                       | 15%                     | 7.5%              |
| 4.0 or more                       | 0%                      | 0%                |

**Example:** A project producing hydrogen with 1.0 kg CO2eq/kg H2 intensity would qualify for 25% credit rate.

### Other Federal Credits

#### Canadian Film or Video Production Tax Credit
**Credit Details:**
- 25% of qualified labour expenditures
- Limited to 15% of total production cost (labour max 60% of total cost)
- Fully refundable
- Cannot claim if film/video production services tax credit claimed for same production

**Documentation:**
- Canadian Film or Video Production Certificate from CAVCO
- Form T1131 for each production

#### Canadian Journalism Labour Tax Credit
**Credit Details:**
- 25% of qualifying labour expenditures (increased to 35% temporarily)
- Maximum $13,750 per eligible newsroom employee annually
- Temporary cap increase to $85,000 per employee (from $55,000)

**Example:** A qualifying journalism organization with $60,000 in qualifying labour expenses per employee could claim $21,000 (35% of $60,000) under the temporary increased rate.
