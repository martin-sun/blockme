# Section 12

**Chapter 12**

---

## T2 Corporation Income Tax Return - Identification Section

### Overview
Page 1 of the T2 return contains essential identification information that enables the CRA to properly identify your corporation and process your return efficiently. Accurate completion of this section is critical for timely processing.

### Email Notifications for T2 Filings

#### Registration Process
Corporations using tax preparation software can register for email notifications by:
1. Providing an email address to the CRA
2. Agreeing to electronic delivery of correspondence when filing

#### Benefits of Registration
- Receive notifications when notices, letters, or statements are available in My Business Account
- Get alerts about important changes to your account
- Reduce paper mail from the CRA (effective June 22, 2023, electronic delivery became the default)

#### Managing Email Preferences
- To register: Visit [canada.ca/my-cra-business-account](https://canada.ca/my-cra-business-account)
- To unsubscribe: Log into My Business Account and select "Notification preferences"
- Note: Separate registration is required for My Business Account

### Business Number (BN) - Line 001

#### Structure
The BN is a 15-character number composed of:
- First 9 characters: Business identifier
- Characters 10-11: Program identifier ("RC" for corporation income tax)
- Last 4 characters: Program account identifier (typically "0001")

#### Implementation Notes
- Enter "0001" as the program account identifier unless advised otherwise by the CRA
- Find your BN on previous notices of assessment, account statements, or remittance forms
- Non-resident corporations must provide incorporation documentation before a BN can be assigned

### Corporation Name - Line 002

Enter the complete legal name of the corporation without abbreviations, ensuring correct punctuation.

### Address Information

#### Head Office Address - Lines 010-018
- Complete if the head office address has changed since last notification to the CRA
- Include street number, street name, city, province/territory/state, and postal/ZIP code
- Note: Cannot be changed through Corporation Internet Filing service

#### Mailing Address - Lines 020-028
- Complete only if different from the head office address
- Update through My Business Account, Represent a Client, or by writing to your tax centre

#### Location of Books and Records - Lines 030-038
- Complete if different from the head office address
- First-time filers after incorporation or amalgamation must complete this section
- Update through My Business Account, Represent a Client, or by writing to your tax centre

### Corporation Type - Lines 040 and 043

#### Classification Options
Select the appropriate box that describes the corporation type at year-end:

1. **Canadian-Controlled Private Corporation (CCPC)**
   - Private corporation resident in Canada
   - Not controlled by non-residents, public corporations, or corporations with shares listed on designated stock exchanges
   - No class of shares listed on a designated stock exchange

2. **Other Private Corporation**
   - Resident in Canada
   - Not a public corporation
   - Not controlled by public corporations or prescribed federal Crown corporations

3. **Public Corporation**
   - Resident in Canada with a class of shares listed on a designated Canadian stock exchange
   - Or has elected/been designated as a public corporation under Regulation 4800(1)

4. **Corporation Controlled by a Public Corporation**
   - Canadian subsidiary of a public corporation

5. **Other Corporation**
   - Does not fall into other categories (e.g., general insurers, Crown corporations)

#### Special Notes
- Credit unions or cooperative corporations should select box 1 if they meet the CCPC definition
- If corporation type changed during the tax year, provide the effective date on line 043
- A change in corporation type may have significant tax consequences

### Tax Year Information - Lines 060, 061, 063, 065

#### Tax Year Start and End - Lines 060 and 061
- Enter the first and last days of the tax year
- A fiscal period cannot exceed 53 weeks (371 days)
- The first day of this tax year must be the day after the last day of the previous tax year
- New corporations may choose any year-end within 53 weeks of incorporation/amalgamation

#### Time Specification
When using Corporation Internet Filing, you can specify hours and minutes for:
- Tax year-end (line 061) when changed due to:
  - Acquisition of control (line 063)
  - Change from CCPC to another corporation type (line 066)
  - First year after amalgamation (line 071)
  - Final year before amalgamation (line 076)
- Tax year start (line 060) when previous year-end had specified time
- Date of acquisition of control (line 065)

#### Example: Multiple Amalgamation on Same Day
Two predecessor corporations amalgamate on January 15, 2024, at 09:44:
- Predecessors report:
  - Line 060 (TYS): 2024-01-01 00:00
  - Line 061 (TYE): 2024-01-15 09:43
- New corporation (final to amalgamation at 17:27):
  - Line 060 (TYS): 2024-01-15 09:44
  - Line 061 (TYE): 2024-01-15 17:26

#### Acquisition of Control - Lines 063 and 065
- Answer yes if a person or group acquired control during the tax year
- Enter the acquisition date on line 065
- Tax year ends immediately before control is acquired under subsection 249(4)
- No approval needed for the changed tax year

#### Deemed Tax Year-End - Line 066
- Answer yes if the corporation becomes or stops being a CCPC (not due to acquisition of control)
- Tax year is deemed to end immediately before the change under subsection 249(3.1)
- No approval needed for the changed tax year

### Special Corporation Statuses

#### Professional Corporation in Partnership - Line 067
- Professional corporations include those practicing accounting, dentistry, law, medicine, veterinary medicine, or chiropractic
- Note: Billed-basis accounting is no longer permitted for tax years beginning after March 21, 2017
- Transitional phase-in of work-in-progress inclusions applies over 5 years

#### First-Time Filer Status - Lines 070, 071, 072
- Line 070: First year after incorporation
- Line 071: First year after amalgamation
- Line 072: Wind-up of subsidiary under section 88
- If yes to any, must file Schedule 24 with the return
- First-year tax period cannot exceed 53 weeks from incorporation/amalgamation date

#### Final Return Status - Lines 076, 078
- Line 076: Final tax year before amalgamation
- Line 078: Final return up to dissolution
- For dissolution, include Schedule 100 showing asset distribution
- Responsible representatives should obtain a clearance certificate before distributing property

### Functional Currency Election - Line 079

#### Eligibility
Corporations resident in Canada throughout the tax year can elect to report in a functional currency, except for:
- Investment corporations
- Mortgage investment corporations
- Mutual fund corporations

#### Qualifying Currencies
- British pound
- Euro
- Australian dollar
- U.S. dollar
- Japanese yen

#### Election Process
- File Form T1296 within the first 61 days of the tax year
- Once elected, cannot change functional currency
- Still must complete line 840 in Canadian currency

### Residency Information - Lines 080-082

#### Non-Resident Corporations
- If not resident in Canada, enter country of residence on line 081
- File Schedule 97 with the return
- Mail returns to the Sudbury Tax Centre (unless filing electronically)
- If claiming treaty exemption, file Schedule 91

### Tax-Exempt Status - Line 085

#### Exemption Categories
Select the appropriate exemption box:

1. **Paragraph 149(1)(e) or (l)**
   - Agricultural organizations, boards of trade, chambers of commerce
   - Clubs, societies, or associations for social welfare, civic improvement, pleasure or recreation
   - May require filing Form T1044 if:
     - Received more than $10,000 in taxable dividends, interest, rentals, or royalties
     - Total assets exceeded $200,000 at end of previous tax year
     - Required to file T1044 for a previous tax year

2. **Paragraph 149(1)(j)**
   - Non-profit corporation for scientific research and experimental development (SR&ED)
   - Must meet specific conditions regarding purpose, income distribution, and expenditures

3. **Other Paragraphs of Section 149**
   - Attach relevant information specifying the exemption paragraph
